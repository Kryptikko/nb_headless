## Ideation (diamond 1)
### Step 1. Purpose and Goal 
Describe core intent of the mechanic.
Describe in abstract terms what player behaviour and theme does the mechanic aim to represent.
Describe how should it shape player behaviour.
What should this mechanic achieve?

Example:
Give the player sense of resiliance.
Give the player a way to manage exposure to magical effects without making them immune. 
Introduce more tactical depth.
It should impact player choices when encountering spell casters, player should decide which spell they should resist
and when they'll take it in the face.

### Step 2. Mechanic Core System 
How is it implemented
Example:
  - dice roll
  - point tracking
How does it fit into with excising mechanics.
Focus on player action, new fresh expiriences, without complex rule changes.

Example:
Player has a limited number of Resistance Points.
Each time a player is targeted by a magical effect the player can choose to spend a point and nullify some of the effect.

### Step 3. Establish the Parameters
Balance the mechanic, 
  - establish sinks and taps
  - thresholds
  - scales

Example:
Player can have up to a maximum of 3 points at a time.
The points reset on short rest.
Spending a point allows the player to roll a d20. Success allows to resist half the spell effect, failure means the spell acts as normal.
